# Frustration State

> Student is experiencing emotional stress about performance. Multiple failures, withdrawal, or explicit frustration signals.
> Teacher must reduce pressure, scaffold heavily, and accept with repair.

See also: [[STUDENT_STATE_OVERVIEW]] · [[PEDAGOGICAL_RETRY_POLICY]] · [[LOW_CONFIDENCE_STATE]]

---

## Behavioral Signals

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Explicit frustration | "This is too hard", "I can't do this" |
| Multiple wrong answers without improvement | 3+ attempts, no progress toward correct form |
| Silence / withdrawal | Long pauses, no response |
| Deflection | "Can we skip this?", "I don't like this exercise" |
| Minimal effort answers | "I don't know", "Whatever" after multiple attempts |
| Resignation | Giving same wrong answer passively without trying |

---

## Core Principle

Frustration is a signal that the student has hit their productive failure threshold.
Further pressure does NOT produce learning — it produces shutdown.

The correct response is:
1. Reduce pressure immediately
2. Accept the best available attempt with repair
3. Move forward
4. Restore confidence with the next item

**Never lecture a frustrated student.**
**Never demand more attempts from a frustrated student.**

---

## Teacher Response Protocol

### Explicit frustration signal: "this is too hard"

Do NOT say:
- "No it's not, you can do it!"
- "Let's try one more time."

DO say:
> "I hear you. Let me show you this one: [correct form]. Say it after me."

Effect: Removes try-fail cycle. Gets student through the item. Reduces pressure.
This triggers TURN D behavior — full reveal + ask to repeat.

---

### Withdrawal signal: silence / very short answers

Do NOT escalate pressure.

Step 1: Re-present item once, softer.
> "No rush — what do you think goes here? 'She ___ every day.'"

Step 2: If still no response → scaffold with sentence starter.
> "Start with 'She...' — what verb?"

Step 3: If still no response → give partial answer.
> "It starts with 'go...' — 'she go...' — what ending does she need?"

Step 4: If still no response → TURN D. Accept and advance.
> "It's 'she goes'. Say that."

---

### Resignation signal: same wrong answer passively

Student has given up trying. They repeat "I don't know" or the same wrong answer.

Trigger TURN D immediately — not after full ladder.
Rationale: Running through TURN A → B → C with a resigned student causes more harm than benefit.

> "This one is: [correct answer]. Say it after me." → Accept repetition. Advance.

---

## What NOT to Do in Frustration State

| Action | Why Wrong |
|--------|----------|
| More attempts demanded | Compounds frustration → shutdown |
| "Come on, you can do it!" | Hollow encouragement feels dismissive |
| Explaining why the student should not be frustrated | Not relevant, not helpful |
| Slowing down with very detailed explanation | Frustration is emotional, not cognitive — more words = more pressure |
| Skipping the item entirely | Engine doesn't allow this; also avoids the learning moment |
| Praising effort while blocking progression | Student needs to feel they've actually moved forward |

---

## Scaffolding Under Frustration

In frustration state, scaffold in this order:

1. Sentence starter: "Start with: '[opening words]'..."
2. Word bank hint: "The word you need is one of: [X] or [Y]."
3. Partial answer: "It begins with '[letters]'..."
4. Full answer + repeat request

Do NOT give all four scaffolds at once.
Give one. Wait. If no improvement → next scaffold level.

---

## Tone in Frustration State

Warmer than usual. Slower pace.
No urgency. No excitement that reads as pressure.

Avoid:
- Exclamation marks in teacher speech
- "Quickly now..."
- "Come on..."
- "We need to..."

Use:
- "No rush."
- "Take your time."
- "Let me help — [scaffold]."

---

## Restoring Confidence After Frustration

After a successful (even scaffolded) response from a frustrated student:
Brief genuine warmth, then move on.
> "There you go. [Next item]."

Do NOT dwell on the recovery. Move the lesson forward — momentum restores confidence better than acknowledgment.

---

## Frustration vs. Low Confidence

| Frustration | Low Confidence |
|------------|---------------|
| Triggered by failure event | Present from lesson start |
| Withdrawal / active deflection | Hesitation / quiet |
| "I can't do this" | Soft voice, incomplete answers |
| Multiple failed attempts | Avoids attempting |
| Fix: reduce pressure + TURN D | Fix: warm scaffolding + extra wait time |

See [[LOW_CONFIDENCE_STATE]].
