# Pronunciation Struggle State

> Student knows the grammar and meaning but cannot produce the target sound correctly.
> Teacher must interpret intent, not penalize phonology. Isolate the pronunciation issue separately.

See also: [[STUDENT_STATE_OVERVIEW]] · [[STT_NOISE_PATTERNS]] · [[VOICE_PEDAGOGY_DOCTRINE]]

---

## Behavioral Signals

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Correct grammar, wrong sound | "She goez to school" (correct grammar, /z/ issue) |
| Repeated attempts at same word | "studies... stu-dees... studease" |
| Correct meaning via STT but phonetically mangled | STT: "sturdy's" → student meant "studies" |
| Student says "I can't say it" | Explicit phonological difficulty |
| Long pause before a specific word | Anticipating difficulty with a sound |
| Self-correction targeting pronunciation not grammar | "inspires... in-SPIRES" (stress attempt) |

---

## Core Principle

**Pronunciation struggle ≠ wrong answer.**

If the student's grammatical content is correct and the meaning is clear:
- The answer is CORRECT
- `allowProgression = true`
- Teacher may note the pronunciation AFTER accepting

**Never mark a grammatically correct answer as wrong because of accent or pronunciation.**

The exercise tests grammar or vocabulary — not phonological production unless it IS a pronunciation exercise.

---

## Teacher Response Protocol

### Case 1: Grammar correct, pronunciation imperfect

Student: "She studease English every day." (meaning: "She studies English")
STT output: "She studease English every day."

Step 1: Interpretation runtime → "studease" → phonetically close to "studies" → accept
Step 2: Validation → "studies" → CORRECT
Step 3: Teacher response:
> "Good — 'studies'. The ending is '-ies'. Say it: 'stu-dees'."

Note: Teacher acknowledges correct answer FIRST, then addresses pronunciation AFTER.
Do NOT withhold progression for pronunciation.

---

### Case 2: Pronunciation so distorted STT cannot decode

Student: "She [unintelligible] to school."

Step 1: STT output is blank or gibberish for the verb
Step 2: Interpretation runtime cannot extract word
Step 3: Teacher:
> "I didn't catch the verb — what was the word you used? Just the verb."

Ask once. If student says something intelligible that's correct → accept.
If second attempt also fails → TURN A hint. Do not penalize the pronunciation.

---

### Case 3: Pronunciation is the explicit exercise goal

If the current exercise is specifically a pronunciation exercise:
- Pronunciation accuracy IS the target
- Apply correction normally for the phonological target
- But: accept meaning, correct sound production gently

---

## What NOT to Do in Pronunciation Struggle State

| Action | Why Wrong |
|--------|----------|
| Mark answer wrong due to accent | Accent ≠ error |
| Require repeated pronunciation drill mid-exercise | Breaks exercise flow |
| Imitate or mock student pronunciation | Harmful to confidence |
| Correct pronunciation before accepting the content | Grammar/content comes first |
| Give TURN A correction for a pronunciation-only failure | Correction turns are for wrong answers, not wrong sounds |

---

## Pronunciation Correction Timing

**During accuracy exercises (fill-in, grammar):**
1. Accept correct grammar content
2. Brief pronunciation note AFTER acceptance
3. Continue to next item

Do NOT stop the exercise for pronunciation drilling.

**After completing an exercise:**
If multiple pronunciation errors were noted, teacher may address one at session end.
Not mid-exercise, not after every item.

---

## STT Tolerance for Pronunciation

The interpretation runtime handles most phonological variations.
See [[STT_NOISE_PATTERNS]] for specific patterns.

Teacher should trust the interpretation runtime.
If the runtime accepted the answer: teacher confirms and moves forward.
Teacher does NOT second-guess the interpretation runtime's output.

---

## Phonological Difficulty Acknowledgment

For genuinely difficult sounds (th, v/w, r/l for some L1 backgrounds):
> "That sound is tricky — you'll get it with practice. 'Studies.' Now the next sentence."

One-line acknowledgment. No phonetics lecture.
Student pronunciation improves through production, not through teacher explanation of phonetics.

---

## Transition Out of Pronunciation Struggle State

If student produces the word correctly: brief positive acknowledgment.
> "There it is — 'studies'. Good."

If student never produces it correctly: accept the intent and continue.
Do NOT loop on pronunciation. Progress the exercise.
